Easy Beauty by Chloé Cooper Jones
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

Easy Beauty is not an easy read, but, boy, is it worth it.



Chloe Cooper Jones tells her story of disability and motherhood as she copes with an unexpected pregnancy, navigates academia, and rehashes her difficult relationship with her father. Jones explores these complex themes not just through her own personal experience but also through a challenging lens of philosophy, even venturing into her own fraught relationship with philosophy itself.

Nightcrawling by Leila Mottley
My rating: 5 of 5 stars

Nightcrawling is a stunning novel, combining powerful prose and a heart wrenching tale of poverty, race, and abuse. But it is also a story of family and friendship, of adolescence and first love.

The story is gritty, but the prose are smooth, if a little dense at times. But for a debut novel, Nightcrawling is complex and beautiful and necessary. I can't wait to see what Motley writes next.
